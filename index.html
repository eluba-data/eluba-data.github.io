Clinic Sales Analysis

Goal: To uncover actionable insights from sales data of a Hair Restoration Clinic by identifying revenue drivers, team performance trends, seasonal patterns, and long-term business growth.

Approach: First I extracted relevant data from Salesforce and exported the reports as csv files. I proceeded to clean extensively with Power Query and prepare my data for analysis. Using Excel and Power BI, I analyzed transaction-level sales data across products, sales reps, and time dimensions (monthly, quarterly, yearly). The dashboards visualize sales trends, product performance, and team output to support data-driven decisions.

Outcome: This analysis helps pinpoint what’s driving revenue, when to optimize marketing, and who to learn from within the sales team. It supports smarter decisions around inventory, staffing, and product prioritization.

Analysis with R

This dashboard showcases the exploratory data analysis and model performance evaluation of a diabetes prediction model. Built using R and visualized with libraries like GGally and ggplot2, the dashboard provides insights into variable correlations and classification model effectiveness.
Dataset was culled from Kaggle

Key Components:
Exploratory Data Analysis: Pairwise Correlation Plot (GGally)
Displays relationships between variables such as Glucose, BMI, Age, and Blood Pressure, segmented by diabetes status.
Notable observations: Glucose and Age show moderate correlation with diabetes presence.
Differences in variable distributions between diabetic and non-diabetic individuals are visually evident.
Boxplots by Diabetes Status
Highlights variations in key features between diabetic and non-diabetic groups, emphasizing potential predictors.
Heatmap of Feature Correlations
Offers a matrix-style overview of inter-variable correlations. Glucose and BMI appear more strongly associated with other health indicators.

Model Performance Evaluation
ROC Curve(Receiver Operating Characteristic)
Illustrates the trade-off between sensitivity and specificity for the diabetes prediction model.
AUC = 0.821, indicating strong discriminative power of the model.
Scatterplot (Glucose vs. BMI)
Colored by outcome (diabetic vs. non-diabetic), revealing possible clustering trends and supporting feature relevance.

Tools Used
R Programming
Libraries: GGally, ggplot2, pROC, reshape2
Static image export for dashboard compilation
This project demonstrates the ability to perform end-to-end data analysis, visualize key insights, and assess model performance in a clear, interpretable format. Suitable for business insights, academic reporting, or healthcare data science portfolios.

Amazon India Analysis

Goal of the Analysis:
To uncover key revenue drivers, regional sales trends, and product preferences within Amazon's clothing category in India—highlighting opportunities for strategic expansion, targeted marketing, and inventory optimization.

Methodology
Dataset Source: Post-study training data provided by Dataleum.
Tools Used: Excel and Power BI for cleaning, analysis, and interactive visualization. Steps Taken:
Cleaning: Removed nulls, standardized size labels, and ensured consistent date formatting.
Trend Analysis: Tracked revenue performance over time to identify sales spikes.
Geographic Breakdown: Analyzed state- and city-level data to isolate high-performing regions.
Product Segmentation: Grouped clothing types to reveal best-sellers and revenue leaders.
Customer Preference Analysis** Examined sales by size category to understand buying patterns.

Key Takeaway: The Indian fashion e-commerce market is not just growing—it's exploding!

Office Supply Analysis

Goal: The Primary Objective is to evaluate revenue trends, identify top performing products and uncover areas for improvement. Key findings reveal growth opportunities, seasonal trends and insights into sales/revenue patterns.

Approach: I made use of Google Sheets and Power BI, did some cleaning with Power Query in Power BI and was able to analyse sales data across products, segments, and regions. The dashboards visualize sales trends, product performance, and tax information to support data-driven decisions.

Recommendations: Leverage Peak Sales Months (June, October, December) and introduce targeted promotions and stock replenishment strategies leading up to these months to maximize sales (could consider a quarterly subscription model)

Boost underperforming states and segments. Ondo has the least revenue but is highly profitable so increased market penetration there with minimal cost could boost revenue and profit.
